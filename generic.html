Title: Visualizing State-wise Suicides in India

Unveiling the Geographical Patterns

Tableu

Dashboard

Introduction:
Suicide is a complex issue that affects individuals and communities across the globe. In India, understanding the distribution and trends of suicides can provide valuable insights for policymakers, researchers, and support organizations. This visualization aims to shed light on the state-wise distribution of suicides in India, highlighting patterns and variations across different regions.

Data Source:
The data used for this visualization is sourced from National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), Govt of India has shared this dataset under Govt. Open Data License - India. It provides comprehensive information on suicides reported across various states in India over a specific time period. The dataset includes variables such as state, gender, age group, causes, and other relevant details.

Visualization Overview:
The visualization presents an interactive and intuitive representation of state-wise suicides in India. It employs a combination of charts, maps, and filters to provide a comprehensive view of the data. Users can explore different aspects of suicides, such as gender, age group, and causes, and observe variations among the states. State-wise Choropleth Map:

The visualization begins with a choropleth map of India, where each state is color-coded based on the number of suicides reported. Darker shades represent higher suicide rates, while lighter shades indicate lower rates. Users can hover over each state to view detailed statistics and compare suicide rates across different regions. Bar Chart - Top 10 States:

A bar chart displays the top 10 states with the highest number of suicides. This chart provides a quick comparison of the most affected states in terms of absolute numbers. Users can interact with the chart to sort the states by gender, age group, or causes of suicide, providing deeper insights into the patterns within these states. Gender and Age Group Breakdown:

A stacked bar chart illustrates the distribution of suicides based on gender and age groups. It showcases the relative proportions of male and female suicides within each age category. Users can toggle between different age groups to observe variations in suicide rates among different segments of the population. Causes of Suicide:

A donut chart showcases the primary causes of suicides in India. It highlights the major contributing factors such as mental health issues, financial distress, relationship problems, and others. Users can click on each segment of the chart to explore detailed information and understand the prevalence of different causes across states.

Conclusion:
This visualization offers an engaging and informative exploration of state-wise suicides in India. By visually representing the data, it facilitates a deeper understanding of the factors and variations associated with suicide rates across different states. This knowledge can be valuable for policymakers, researchers, and organizations working towards suicide prevention and mental well-being in India.
